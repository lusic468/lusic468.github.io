Josef Albers Color Interaction Exercise

In my original composition, with in mind Albers' theories of color and their interaction, I utilized vivid, saturated colors but each with distinct values. The way I have the squares laid out, all of the colors touch each other, with the darkest, the blue, being nearly surrounded by the others. The turquoise, on par with a midtone, provides a sort of base for the blue and yellow shapes. The yellow square, which has a bright value to it and is tucked behind, acts as a highlight and accent to the two blue squares. The pink square, which is far bigger than all the others and actually contains them, is another base for the vibrant colors to stand on. All of the colors included are of primary colors.
