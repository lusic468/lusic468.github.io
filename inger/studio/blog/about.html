About

Queer theory has a complex, albeit shorter, history that is multilayered and highly intersectional. Due to the development in the ’80s and ’90s of queer theory, contemporary opinions have departed from a more lesbian-gay-attentive ‘LGBT community’ of the 20th century to one that includes the entire queer spectrum, that is people who identify gender—and lack thereof— or orientate sexuality—and lack thereof—beyond what is deemed the heteronorm.


Cobey is a designer and artist born in Austin, Texas currently living in New York City. As an undergraduate Communication Design student attending Parsons The New School for Design, he’s interested in the expansion of multilingual design that demonstrates an equitable, if not completely democratic, interest in diverse audiences in the contexts of publication, branding, or even film.
     Prospectively, Cobey will explore the intersectionality of design and visual language and how that can be implemented with regard to multilingual editorial, typography, and motion graphics.
     For now, he is simply trying to stay well-fed and complete his degree. During his free time, Cobey likes to walk while listening to audiobooks by Jim Dale or songs by The Arcade Fire and the like.
